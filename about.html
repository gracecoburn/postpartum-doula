About Me

Meet Your Postpartum Doula

I am an incredibly blessed mom of two and wife to an amazing husband, we live in Salem Oregon and love our home, the Pacific Northwest. We love biking, hiking and kayaking as a family and I personally enjoy watercoloring and reading, when I have time. I have also taken up gardening in recent years and my brown thumb has turned green! Watching my kids dig around in the dirt and pick their own food while playing in the yard gives me such joy. Now, I have felt the call to help children since I was a young teen but only since having my own have I realized the invaluable role the parents play in the well-being of the family as a whole, this has led me to postpartum work. During my time living in China I first heard about a practice which translates to ‘confinement’; this is a traditional period of time where a new mother rests at home while being cared for by others. She wants for nothing and her only responsibilities are to rest, recuperate and feed her baby. I remember this being presented to me and I thought it sounded amazing! A few years went by and I had my first child and then was recommended the book The First Forty Days by Heng Ou and was reminded of this practice. As I prayed over what I might do when my children started school I was struck by a thought, I knew I needed to give mothers and parents support in their postpartum times. In China was where I also first witnessed elimination communication in action and later looked into the subject when having my first baby. I was able to do this to a degree with my first and even more with my second, saving lots of time, money and stress not having to potty train them in the western way. I was also supported in my desire to use cloth diapers by my family and I am so thankful for the gift that was and found using them partnered great with elimination communication. This journey has led me here and I am so thankful for each family who has welcomed me into their home to serve. Grace
